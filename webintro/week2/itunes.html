Up a level

Musica, Maestro!

Okay, let’s get it out of the way: I love music! All kinds of music: rock, classical, world, ambient, rhythm & blues… You get the picture. And so when Apple released the iPod and iTunes, I got onboard and learned all of the ins and outs. I used iTunes to digitize my CDs and vinyl, and then carefully curated the collection. Cross indexing by artist was only the beginning! And Smart Playlists, OMG! Smart Playlists rock. I also discovered Podcasts and Internet Radio.

What I Use

I upgraded the software and the hardware every couple of years, and was always pleased with the improvements. But I must confess, a few years I stayed with the 5th generation iPod Classic and iTunes 9. As well, I have the Windows version. Although I have a couple of Macs, I moved over to Windows because of my work environment. It wasn’t a problem because the controls on the software are nearly identical.

The only drawback is that the 5th generation can only be associated to one computer. I don’t know if that’s the way it works on newer devices, but mine is completely monogamous: It’s married to a big ol’ honkin’ laptop, a ridiculous thing that I keep only because of it’s glorious 1920x1200 IPS screen. Buyt I digress.

Portability

One of the things I like about iTunes is the portability of the music. I don’t mean just the ability to listen on the iPod or on the laptop. I mean the fact that I can copy the Music folder (and related XML files) to another computer and have my entire library there, including subscribed Podcasts, Radio Stations, and Playlists.

Because I’ve digitized my collections, and also beciase I purchase music through Amazon instead of the iTunes Store, there is no DRM () on my media, so I’m free to copy, store, and play it wherever I want.

Upgrades

Because I have the opprotunity to use a few different computers, I’ve been able to install different versions of iTunes on them. I’ve experimented with the UI (User Interface) as well as the functionality of the various versions, up to and including 12.4.1 as of this writing. And I can honestly say that for ease of use and flexibility, version 9.2 rocks my world.

Wrapping Up

If you have an iPod, I hope I have helped you discover a few more capabilites of this marvelous device, especially when it is paired with iTunes.
